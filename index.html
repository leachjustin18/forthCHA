With a sick child, siblings also experience stress and suffer a wide range of feelings, thoughts and reactions.
  • Siblings may feel left out as parents spend time at the hospital or they may worry that they too will get sick.
  • Siblings may feel they are to blame and their jealous feelings toward the new sibling may be the cause of tragedy.
  • Siblings may believe their bad behavior may be causing their parents' obvious distress.
  • Siblings may also show resentment toward their parents at the perceived loss of attention.
Depending upon the child's age, the sibling will use various coping mechanisms to deal with his or her stress. Regression and acting out are not uncommon. Encourage the parents to discuss how the siblings are reacting and coping with the family crisis and provide referral to social services and family support groups as appropriate. Between two and six years of age, children have magical thinking. They need an explanation of events. They must be told they did nothing to cause their brother or sister's illness.
Children greater than three are interested in babies. For those with a sibling in the NICU, seeing pictures of or looking through the window at their new brother or sister may be helpful. There are books specific to siblings about infants in the NICU.
Children less than four years of age may suffer separation anxiety as their parent's time and focus are on the sick sibling.
It is best that the child care for siblings left at home be provided by a familiar person in a familiar environment.
